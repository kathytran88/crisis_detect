Case Studies

Housing Crisis of 2008

The collapse of the housing market caused by a housing bubble, predatory lending practices, an excessive reliance on subprime mortgage-backed securities, unethical credit ratings, and a high level of mortgage defaults.

Madoff Investment Scandal of 2009

Bernie Madoff orchestrated one of the largest Ponzi schemes in history by attracting investors with the promise of high returns and then using new investors' funds to pay returns to existing clients.

Silicon Valley Bank Collapse of 2023

Silicon Valley Bank's excessive invesment in long-term government bonds caused them to go bankrupt after a bank run because their bonds decreased in value when the Fed raised interest rate in 2023.

Great Depression of 1930

A worldwide economic downturn triggered by the 1929 stock market crash, which resulted from speculative excesses, overleveraging, and a lack of financial regulation.

Dotcom crisis of 1995

Investors' excessive enthusiasm for internet-based businesses led to inflated stock valuations that were not supported by the companies' earnings, causing unsustainable levels of stock prices.

Mexican Pesos crises of 1994

A large current account deficit, high levels of external debt, and a speculative attack on the peso caused the value of the Mexian Pesos to depreciate, leading to Mexico's economic downturn.
